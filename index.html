Intetional Growth

This Week's Scriptures

  • 1 Corinthians 13:10-13

Sunday Sermon Pointers

  • We need to have a firm desire to move forward in our lives.
  • The only thing that requires no effort on our part is growing in age- we cannot stop the clock from ticking.
  • The problem arises when there is a gap between our age and our level of maturity.
  • Change will happen if we allow change in our lives.
  • God continues to place us in positions of change.
  • The bible says that whatever we ask shall be given, the problem is we may not know what to ask from God.
  • We need to be able to grow to where we know what to ask from God.
  • The moment we plateau in our growth process, we start declining.
  • We have been created to be dynamic beings.
  • Moments of stagnation may be an opportunity when God making us think of our next step in life.
  • Today is always a preparation for tomorrow.
  • We have been created perfect by God and He plans to raise us from one level of glow to another.
  • When we compare ourselves to others, we stop growing.
  • It is high time we turn away from that roundabout and focus on our own paths.
  • God is calling us to a new level.
  • Let us stop dreaming of the things we cannot do and start doing the things we can do and see what God will do with us next.
  • Most of the times we stand on our way of growth- we do not trust ourselves enough.

Discussion Questions

  • What is the single thing that you plan to do this year that will matter most in 10 years?
  • What habit would you most like to establish this year?

Growth Mindest

  • Not shying away from opportunities- David did not shy away from stepping up and facing Goliath (1 Samuel 17).
  • Even in the midst of challenges move forward and praise the Lord (James 1:2‐4).
  • Not comparing yourself to others- Leah and Rachel
  • Constantly keeping a positive attitude- Reminding ourselves that we derive strength from God (Phillipians 4:13 )
